Magic in Ka-Tet is not bright, casual high fantasy. It is rarer, stranger, and usually tied to song, psychic talent, relic technology, inherited corruption, or the will of the Beam.

The main power-using archetypes are the **Singer**, **Touch-Bearer**, **Demon-Blooded**, and **Tech-Knight**. The book suggests using a familiar slot-and-level framework for groups that want mechanical clarity, while keeping the fiction grounded in Mid-World style.

## What powers should feel like

- **Singer powers** sound like harmony, grief, command, healing, or a voice that changes the air.
- **Touch-Bearer powers** feel psychic, intuitive, empathic, or prophetic.
- **Demon-Blooded powers** feel dangerous, invasive, or Prim-touched.
- **Tech-Knight powers** often appear as devices, formulas, drones, or revived Old Ones machinery.

## A good table rule

Describe the effect first, then choose the mechanic. The stranger or stronger the effect, the more likely it should leave a signature: static in the teeth, blood from the nose, a wrong note in the air, machine whine, psychic backlash, or unwanted attention from something listening nearby.

Ka-Tet favors expressive powers over encyclopedic spell lists. The rules should help the table decide what a power feels like, what it costs, and what changes in the scene after it lands.
