Combat in Ka-Tet should feel mean, cinematic, and brief. The system rewards planning, positioning, and nerve more than long attrition grinds, and the book is explicit that firearms, cover, and scarcity are what give fights their identity.

## Principles

- Violence escalates quickly.
- Cover is life.
- Fear, panic, and collateral damage are always on the table.

## Core flow

When violence begins, everyone rolls initiative, usually `1d20 + Dexterity`. On your turn you normally get movement, one action, and whatever bonus action or reaction your class features allow. Standard d20 options like Dash, Dodge, Disengage, Grapple, Help, and Use Object all still matter here.

Attack rolls work normally: roll a `d20`, add the right modifiers, and meet or beat Armor Class. Strength usually drives melee attacks, while Dexterity drives most firearms and other ranged attacks.

## What makes these fights different

- Guns hit hard and ammo runs out.
- Armor helps less than smart positioning.
- Cover can matter more than a raw AC number.
- Called shots and stunts should change the scene, not just add color text.

The book suggests a `-5` penalty or disadvantage for a called shot, with a special effect on a hit such as disarming an enemy, slowing a target, or striking a machine's weak point.

## Going down

Grit can represent real injury, near misses, exhaustion, or sheer will to keep moving. At `0 HP`, a character drops and starts dying unless a rule, power, or Ka Point changes that outcome. Healing and rest matter because even when the group survives, the road keeps the bill.

If a scene feels like a stand-up slugfest, add movement, civilians, environmental danger, or a clock.
