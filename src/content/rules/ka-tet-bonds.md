Ka-Tet Bonds keep the campaign from feeling like parallel solo stories. They reward the table for making the party matter as much as the plot.

The book's core idea is simple: the player party is assumed to be a ka-tet, "one made from many." That means the bond is not just emotional color. It is one of the setting's most important truths. The group is supposed to share danger, intuition, and a sense that fate wants them on the same road.

## What the bond means in play

- The party has stronger reasons to stay together than convenience alone.
- Shared dreams, instinctive trust, and moments of wordless understanding are normal.
- Protecting the tet is usually worth more than protecting pride.

The lore also points to **khef**, a kind of shared breath or life-force. A deep ka-tet may finish each other's thoughts, feel distant pain, or sense when something is wrong with one of their own.

## Mechanical use

This page works best when each character starts with at least one bond statement tied to another member of the group. Invoke that bond when you protect them, tell them a truth you were hiding, suffer in their place, or act in line with the relationship instead of in spite of it.

The book also supports broader ka-tet mechanics such as pooled inspiration, linked Ka Point spending, or chain rerolls that show luck moving through the group instead of stopping at one person.

## When the bond hurts

Ka-tets are not always gentle. The bond can carry grief, obligation, jealousy, or ka-shume, the terrible sense that a break in the tet is coming. Use that weight to deepen scenes, not to excuse party sabotage.
