Ka Points are the signature pressure valve of the game. They let the table say, "this moment matters enough for fate to lean on it."

## What Ka does best

- Smooths over a brutal streak of bad luck
- Rewards players who reinforce the party bond
- Gives weird, desperate scenes a mythic push

## Earning Ka

The GM should hand out Ka when players honor promises, choose sacrifice over ease, follow their destiny, hit major story milestones, or pull each other through impossible situations. The book also suggests awarding Ka after heavy losses or scenes where the Beam or the ka-tet bond clearly shaped events.

In most campaigns, Ka should feel meaningful rather than constant. A good baseline is roughly one point per player every session or two when the fiction justifies it.

## Spending Ka

- Spend **1 Ka** to reroll a `d20` after you see the result but before you know the outcome.
- Spend **1 Ka** to ask for a stroke of luck, clue, or useful coincidence when the table is stuck.
- Spend **1 Ka** for a heroic surge if the GM agrees the moment deserves it.
- Spend **2 Ka** to turn a deadly hit into survival, such as downgrading a critical or staying at `1 HP`.

## Refresh, sharing, and limits

Characters usually begin with **1 Ka Point**. The pool is intentionally small, and the default cap is **3 Ka Points**. If you are completely empty, a session start or long period of safe downtime can restore a point of luck so fate is never gone for long.

Ka is personal, but the tet can sometimes share it. The book supports donating a point to another player when the bond is clear in the fiction, reinforcing that the group is stronger when it acts like a ka-tet instead of a pile of solo heroes.

## Dark Ka

Groups that want a sharper horror edge can track Dark Ka separately. The GM can spend it to hand enemies a lucky break, introduce a jam, or make a complication hit at the worst possible moment. It is optional, and it works best when used sparingly.
