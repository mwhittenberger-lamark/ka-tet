Gunslingers matter because they embody a hard question: can discipline and honor survive after institutions rot away?

In the old days, gunslingers were Mid-World's knight-errants, peacekeepers, and living symbols of the White. They carried legendary revolvers, followed a severe code, and were expected to lead when ordinary authority failed.

By the default era of the game, the order is mostly gone. What remains are heirs, imitators, deserters, legends, and the dangerous weight of a code that still means something to people living among ruins.
