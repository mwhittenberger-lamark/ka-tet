Todash space is useful to explain sparingly. It is more effective as a feeling than as a lecture: pressure in the ears, static in the teeth, and the sense that distance has stopped meaning what it used to.

It is the unstable between-space reached through certain doors, rituals, relics, and disasters. The book frames it as a black limbo between worlds, full of distance, hungry things, and the constant risk of getting lost.

Characters can brush against Todash space by accident or go there deliberately with the wrong kind of courage. Either way, it should feel uncanny, dangerous, and only partly survivable.
