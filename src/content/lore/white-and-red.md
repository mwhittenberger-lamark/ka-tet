The White represents alignment toward protection, restoration, and costly responsibility. The Red is hunger, control, corruption, and the argument that pain is the truest law.

The book treats these less like tidy religions and more like cosmic directions. The White is tied to mercy, creation, sacrifice, and the effort to keep the world from collapsing. The Red is tied to the Crimson King's influence, domination, decay, and the temptation to treat power as the only truth that matters.

Characters do not need to pledge themselves formally for these forces to shape their story. Every choice about duty, cruelty, mercy, or corruption points one way or the other.
